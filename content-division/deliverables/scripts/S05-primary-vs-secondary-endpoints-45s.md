# S05 — "Primary vs Secondary Endpoints: What a Trial Is Really Testing" (Evergreen · 45s · Trial Design Teardown)

**Format:** Trial Design Teardown · **Length:** 45s (~120 words) · **Ticker:** none
**Source:** ClinicalTrials.gov / trial-design fundamentals · **Disclosure flags:** educational
**On-camera:** Kevin hook + payoff; Avatar for the bullseye metaphor.

---

**[HOOK — Kevin, 0–3s]**
"A trial can 'work' and still fail. It all comes down to the endpoint it promised to hit."

**[BODY — Avatar, 3–34s]**
"Every trial names a **primary endpoint** up front — the one result it's designed and powered to prove. **[avatar aims dart at center of bullseye]** That's the shot that counts. **[FACT chip]**

**Secondary endpoints** are the extra targets — supportive, interesting, but they don't save a trial that misses the primary. **[smaller outer rings]**

So when a company leads its press release with a *secondary* result and stays quiet about the primary? **[avatar raises eyebrow]** That's a tell."

**[TURN — Avatar, 34–40s]**
"Read the primary endpoint first, every time. Then check if it was actually met, not just 'trended positive'."

**[PAYOFF + CTA — Kevin, 40–44s]**
"Judge the trial by the shot it called. Follow for the next teardown."
