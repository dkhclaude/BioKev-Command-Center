# S03 — "How Long Before This Biotech Runs Out of Money" (Evergreen · 60s · Cash Runway Check)

**Format:** Cash Runway Check · **Length:** 60s (~160 words) · **Ticker:** none
**Source:** SEC 10-Q/10-K methodology · **Disclosure flags:** educational
**On-camera:** Kevin hook + payoff; Avatar for the runway-plane metaphor + math.

---

**[HOOK — Kevin, 0–3s]**
"Most early biotechs make zero dollars in revenue. So the real question isn't 'is the drug good' — it's 'do they have enough cash to get there?'"

**[BODY — Avatar, 3–46s]**
"This is cash runway, and you can estimate it from one filing. **[avatar at whiteboard]**

Step one: find cash and equivalents plus short-term investments — total liquidity. **[FACT chip]**
Step two: find quarterly cash burn — roughly how much cash operations eat per quarter.
Step three: divide. Liquidity ÷ burn = quarters of runway. **[plane taxiing down a shrinking runway]**

If they've got 4 quarters left and a big trial readout in 5, guess what's coming? A raise. **[avatar pours water into 'your shares' — dilution gag]**

Companies also give a 'runway through' date — treat that as *their estimate*, and check it yourself."

**[TURN — Avatar, 46–53s]**
"Short runway before a catalyst = high odds of dilution, often at a bad price."

**[PAYOFF + CTA — Kevin, 53–58s]**
"Always price the raise before the readout. Follow for the next teardown."

**[DISCLOSURE — on-screen 55–60s]**
"Educational only. Not investment or medical advice."
