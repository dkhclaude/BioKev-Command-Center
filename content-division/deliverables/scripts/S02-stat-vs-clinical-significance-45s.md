# S02 — "Statistically Significant ≠ Actually Better" (Evergreen · 45s · Myth vs Mechanism)

**Format:** Myth vs Mechanism · **Length:** 45s (~118 words) · **Ticker:** none
**Source:** general biostatistics / FDA guidance · **Disclosure flags:** educational
**On-camera:** Kevin hook + payoff; Avatar for the trophy-vs-patient metaphor.

---

**[HOOK — Kevin, 0–3s]**
"A biotech says 'we hit statistical significance' and the stock rips. Sometimes that's meaningless. Here's why."

**[BODY — Avatar, 3–33s]**
"'Statistically significant' — usually p less than 0.05 — just means the result probably isn't random chance. **[avatar holds tiny p<0.05 trophy]** That's it.

It does NOT tell you if the effect is big enough to matter to a patient. That's **clinical** significance. **[avatar gestures to a patient who feels better]**

You can have a drug that beats placebo by a hair — statistically real, clinically pointless. And regulators and doctors care about the second one."

**[TURN — Avatar, 33–39s]**
"So when a readout drops, don't just ask 'did it hit?' Ask 'by how much, and does that change a life?'"
