**[HOOK — Kevin, 0–3s]**
"Before some drugs get approved, a panel of outside experts votes in public. It can tank or launch a stock in an afternoon."

**[BODY — Avatar, 3–34s]**
"It's called an advisory committee — an AdComm. The FDA gathers independent experts to review the data and vote on questions like 'do the benefits outweigh the risks?' **[avatar as referee panel raising cards]** **[FACT chip]**

Two things to know. One: the vote is **non-binding** — the FDA usually follows it, but doesn't have to. Two: the FDA posts its own briefing documents a couple days before. **[avatar reads a leaked-looking dossier]** Those docs alone can move the stock before anyone even votes."

**[TURN — Avatar, 34–40s]**
"So an AdComm is really *two* events: the briefing docs, then the vote."

**[PAYOFF + CTA — Kevin, 40–44s]**
