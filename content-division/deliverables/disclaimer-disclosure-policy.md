# BioKev — Disclaimer & Disclosure Policy

**Applies to:** every video, carousel, newsletter, and post published under the BioKev brand.
**Owner:** Compliance & Credibility Editor (Agent 05). Nothing publishes without meeting this policy.

> Note: this is an operational policy, not legal advice. Kevin should have a licensed attorney review the standing disclaimers and his position-disclosure approach before scaling, especially given his PharmD license.

## 1. Core standing disclaimer (all content)
> "BioKev is educational biotech research and commentary. It is not investment advice, financial advice, or a recommendation to buy or sell any security. It is not medical advice. I am a pharmacist, not your financial advisor or your doctor. Do your own research and consult a licensed professional. I may hold positions in securities I discuss."

- **Video:** on-screen ≥3s ("Educational only. Not investment or medical advice.") + full version in caption/description.
- **Newsletter/LinkedIn/blog:** full version in footer of every issue/post.

## 2. Position disclosure (financial-conflict)
- Any time a specific ticker is named, disclose Kevin's position: **long / short / none / planning to open within 72h.**
- On-screen: yellow "Position: [x]" chip. In caption: one line.
- If Kevin plans to trade around a catalyst he's covering, disclose it and avoid posting content that could look like pump-and-dump timing.

## 3. Fact / Assumption / Opinion separation (required)
Every claim is one of three, and must be worded (and, on screen, color-coded) accordingly:
- **Fact** (yellow): sourced to FDA / SEC / ClinicalTrials.gov / peer-review / IR. Verbs: "confirmed", "reported", "the label states".
- **Assumption / estimate** (pink dashed): "the company estimates", "consensus expects", "likely". Estimated dates carry an "EST." tag in audio and on screen.
- **Opinion** (gray "OPINION" chip): "my read", "I think", "I'd watch". Never stated as fact.

## 4. Medical-advice guardrails
- No dosing, treatment, or "you should take" statements to viewers.
- Discuss therapies at the population/mechanism level, educationally.
- Add "not medical advice" whenever a specific therapy or condition is discussed.
- Do not imply efficacy/safety beyond what the cited data supports; distinguish investigational vs approved.

## 5. Financial-advice guardrails
- No price targets stated as fact, no "buy/sell", no "guaranteed", no return promises.
- No fabricated dates, endpoints, cash figures, or analyst targets — ever.
- Distinguish statistical vs clinical significance whenever data is discussed.
- Educational trade setups (if any) are labeled as scenarios with explicit invalidation conditions and "subject to your own research".

## 6. Sourcing & accuracy
- Every factual claim traces to a primary source; record event date AND publication date when they differ.
- Corrections policy: if a video contains a material error, post a correction and pin it; note it in the next newsletter's scorecard.

## 7. Sponsorship / paid content (if ever)
- Any paid or affiliate content is clearly labeled "#ad / sponsored" per FTC guidance.
- Never accept payment to promote a specific stock. Company-sponsored *education* (if ever) must be disclosed and cannot alter the honest bull/bear framing.

## 8. AI-content transparency
- The BioKev avatar and AI-generated visuals are clearly a stylized cartoon; the real Kevin appears on camera. No AI is used to fabricate quotes, data, or a real person's likeness.

## 9. Pre-publish checklist (Agent 05 sign-off)
- [ ] Standing disclaimer present (on-screen ≥3s + caption)
- [ ] Position chip if a ticker is named
- [ ] Every claim labeled Fact/Assumption/Opinion
- [ ] Estimated dates tagged EST.
- [ ] No price target / buy-sell / dosing language
- [ ] Sources cited, dates recorded
- [ ] Stat vs clinical significance not conflated
- [ ] Approved vs investigational not conflated
